About Me

I'm a student in my fourth year of a six year IT (computer science) and Law double degree at QUT in Brisbane. At the moment I am most interested broadly in software and solving problems using programming and computation. While I don't see myself becoming a lawyer, I am interested in opportunities to combine my fields however. Therefore things like copyright/IP law, internet law, internet rights, privacy, government intervention, and so on, all interest me.

I’d describe myself as relaxed, level-headed and cool under pressure but with a great work ethic and initiative. I love to be organised and have things planned in advanced but I’m just as willing to improvise and be quick to an idea when the time calls for it or when things don’t go to plan. Always good for a joke and a believer in clear, open, and honest communication, I work well in a team setting or alone when required or when I am able. Overall I think my key strengths are my persistence, aptitude for learning, creativity in the face of problems, and love for what I do.

My goal is to finish my formal education and then find work somewhere where I can be challenged, keep learning, build cool stuff, and solve problems to make the world and people’s lives better.
For now though, I’m focusing on my studies and getting as much experience and knowledge as I can while also trying to find time for travel, hobbies and generally staying well rounded. In my spare time I enjoy a variety of sport and activities, playing music, traveling, camping, reading, and of course programming and playing with technology.

Boeing Defence Australia

Student Software Engineer - Since December 2015

I started out in this role as part of Boeing's 10 week summer internship program and was fortunate enough to be offered ongoing casual work along with my university studies.

I am part of a software engineering team and work along side the other engineers to complete the projects and tasks assigned to us. Loosely, the team's current work involves prototyping aspects of an existing system onto more modern technology.

Working on a real project is a great experience and, within that, is also my greatest challenge. In university there is often a comforting framework to support you in assignment work and so I have found it refreshing, challenging and extremely enjoyable to be able to work on real problems where the answer or solution isn't known. I love building and creating so I get immense satisfaction from being given a problem, taking it away, and finding a solution.
